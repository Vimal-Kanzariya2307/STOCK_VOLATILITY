What is the Decay Factor (λ)?

The decay factor λ controls the weight distribution of the exponential variance model. A smaller value (e.g. 0.90) places heavier weight on the most recent daily daily returns, making the forecast highly reactive to sudden market shocks. A higher value (e.g. 0.97) smooths the model, placing more reliance on long-term historical volatility behavior.

EWMA Risk Model

The Exponentially Weighted Moving Average (EWMA) model computes volatility by applying exponential weights that decrease over time. Unlike basic standard deviation models, it reacts quickly to changes in price volatility.

σ_t² = λ · σ_{t-1}² + (1 - λ) · r_t²
📈

Rolling Volatility

Calculates standard deviation of daily log returns over a rolling 30-day window, annualized by assuming 252 trading days. This provides an unweighted historical average of asset volatility.

Volatility = StdDev(r) · √252
📊

Moving Average Cross

Monitors trend direction using Simple Moving Averages. A Golden Cross (MA50 crossing above MA100) generates a Bullish signal. A Death Cross (MA50 crossing below MA100) indicates a Bearish trend.

MA_N = (1 / N) · Σ_{i=0}^{N-1} Close_{t-i}
